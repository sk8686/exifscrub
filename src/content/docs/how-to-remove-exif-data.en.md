## Remove EXIF Data on iPhone

iOS has built-in EXIF removal when sharing:

1. Open the **Photos** app
2. Select the photo(s) you want to share
3. Tap the **Share** button
4. Tap **Options** at the top
5. Toggle **Location** off
6. Share the photo

**Limitation**: This only removes GPS data, not camera model or timestamps. For complete removal, use our [online tool](/).

## Remove EXIF Data on Android

Android doesn't have built-in EXIF removal. Your options:

- Use our [web-based tool](/) — works on any Android browser
- Install a dedicated app like "Photo EXIF Editor" from the Play Store
- Some gallery apps have metadata editing features

## Remove EXIF Data on Windows

1. Right-click the image → **Properties**
2. Go to the **Details** tab
3. Click **Remove Properties and Personal Information**
4. Choose "Create a copy with all possible properties removed"
5. Click **OK**

**Limitation**: Windows can't remove all EXIF tags. For thorough cleaning, use our [browser-based tool](/).

## Remove EXIF Data on macOS

1. Open the image in **Preview**
2. Go to **Tools** → **Show Inspector** (⌘I)
3. Click the **(i)** tab, then the **Exif** tab
4. Click **Remove Metadata** at the bottom

**Limitation**: Preview doesn't remove all metadata types. For complete removal, use our [online tool](/).

## What Gets Removed vs. Preserved

**Removed**: GPS coordinates, camera make/model, timestamps, serial numbers, software info, thumbnails, copyright info

**Preserved**: Image orientation (prevents wrong rotation), color profile (keeps colors accurate), image dimensions, actual pixel data

## When Should You Remove EXIF Data?

Always remove EXIF data before:
- Posting photos on social media (some platforms strip it, many don't)
- Sending photos to strangers via email or messaging apps
- Uploading photos to forums or classified sites
